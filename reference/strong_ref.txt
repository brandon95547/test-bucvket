The cathedral stood abandoned beneath a sky that had long forgotten the warmth of dawn. Its weathered stone walls drank in the moonlight, while shattered stained glass scattered ghostly colors across the cold floor like the remnants of forgotten souls. Every step echoed through the endless halls, awakening whispers that seemed to rise from the very foundations, carrying the weight of prayers left unanswered for centuries. The air was thick with the scent of damp earth, extinguished candles, and ancient dust, as though time itself had been entombed within those sacred walls.

Beyond the broken altar, a forest of twisted yew trees stretched into an ocean of darkness where no stars dared to shine. Their skeletal branches clawed endlessly at the heavens, swaying without the touch of wind, while pale mist coiled between the roots like restless spirits searching for names they could no longer remember. Ravens watched in perfect silence from blackened limbs, their unblinking eyes reflecting a light that belonged to neither moon nor fire, but to something far older that slumbered beneath the forgotten soil.
