The cathedral stood abandoned beneath a sky that had long forgotten the warmth of dawn. Its weathered stone walls drank in the moonlight, while shattered stained glass scattered ghostly colors across the cold floor like the remnants of forgotten souls. Every step echoed through the endless halls, awakening whispers that seemed to rise from the very foundations, carrying the weight of prayers left unanswered for centuries. The air was thick with the scent of damp earth, extinguished candles, and ancient dust, as though time itself had been entombed within those sacred walls.

Yet beneath the silence, something endured. Deep within the cathedral's forgotten chambers, beyond crumbling archways and rusted iron gates, a presence waited with endless patience. It did not breathe, nor did it sleep, but listened to every creak of ancient timbers and every distant heartbeat that dared cross the threshold. Those who entered seeking answers often found themselves wandering in circles, drawn ever deeper by voices that knew their names, until the boundary between memory and madness dissolved into the darkness. Only the faint glow of a dying candle remained to mark where the last traveler had stood before vanishing into the shadows forever.
